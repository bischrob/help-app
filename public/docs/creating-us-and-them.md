# Creating Us and Them

Around the world, people divide each other up in different ways. For example, in the United States, people often classify themselves and others as "White", "Black", "Asian". These are broad racial categories based on people's judgments about several factors, including where people's ancestors came from and what physical features they share. In other countries, they often make other kinds of distinctions. For example, in India, people are often grouped into hereditary social groups, called castes, defined by a combination of traditional occupation and social status. In other places, other aspects of a group's common origin, such as religion or language play an important part in distinguishing between groups. We will use **ethnicity** as a broad term for groups that have been defined in terms of a common identity and origin, whether that is based on region, hereditary occupation, religion, or language.

How and why do different societies divide people up based on these different criteria? SocioMap (<https://catmapper.org/sociomap>) provides useful tools for exploring this question across over 25000 ethnicities. In this exercise, we will use SocioMap to examine two questions:

1. What are the ways that different societies distinguish between different ethnicities?

2. What are the names that members of ethnic groups call themselves and what names are given to them by others?
